Church News interviews Elder David P. Homer about the updated Sunday schedule beginning in September, where the second hour includes weekly Sunday School plus weekly Relief Society, elders quorum, youth, and Primary classes in two 25-minute blocks. He frames the change as a way to strengthen home-centered, church-supported gospel learning, deepen belonging, and help members become lifelong disciples of Jesus Christ.

What this video offers

The schedule is meant to connect Come, Follow Me study at home with weekly discussion at church for adults, youth, and children.

Teachers and leaders should use the shorter class time deliberately by moving quickly into scripture, conference talks, and personal application rather than lengthy opening routines.

Relief Society and elders quorum will be able to study more general conference messages, using counseling skills to discuss what the doctrine means and how to live it.

Youth lessons will use the expanded For the Strength of Youth guide, with fast Sunday and last Sunday lessons anchoring each monthly topic.

Ward and stake councils should study the implementation resources, prepare for the August 30 and September 6 training materials, and keep sacrament meeting centered on Jesus Christ.
