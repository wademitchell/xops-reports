TL;DR
Andrew Warner and Corey Ganim run through a week of practical AI launches focused on repeatable workflows, agents inside team tools, and cheaper model selection. The strongest theme is that AI products are shifting from flashy demos toward work that can be recorded, delegated, automated, and reused inside tools teams already use.
Creator's Key Callouts
  • Record & Replay in Codex is framed as one of the most impactful updates in a long time because it can turn a screen-recorded workflow into a reusable skill.
  • Claude Tag and WorkClaw show the move toward AI agents living directly inside Slack and Teams, but the hosts warn teams may need to manage notification spam and automated slop.
  • Zapier MCP stands out for safer, scoped permissions when giving agents access to email, calendar, Drive, and other tools.
  • Blue Rails points toward agent-readable and agent-actionable websites, which may matter as AI agents become traffic and transaction drivers.
  • OpenRouter MCP is useful for people who care about token spend and want live guidance on the best or cheapest model for a specific task.
