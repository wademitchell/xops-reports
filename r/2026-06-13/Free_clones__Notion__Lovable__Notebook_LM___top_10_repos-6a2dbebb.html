TL;DR
Andrew Warner and guest Peter Cooper walk through the week's hottest open-source AI repos on GitHub. Highlights include a "last 30 days" research skill, a token-compression tool for agents, a local NotebookLM clone with custom voices, and a design-taste skill that breaks the generic-Claude look.
Creator's Key Callouts
  • Andrew's #1 pick is last30days-skill. He ran it live to write a cold email pitch to Greg Eisenberg and loved that it pulled fresh data from X, Reddit, and the web.
  • Headroom is "back this week" because of Fable's token burn. Especially useful when debugging or working with verbose JSON.
  • Tolaria is Andrew's personal standout: looks like Notion, runs locally, and works directly on your filesystem so agents can edit notes without using the app's UI.
  • Peter notes that taste-skill is a "trade one set of cliches for another" solution. He recommends copying the style files and remixing them into your own design system.
  • The host used a comedic anthropic-mocked video at the end to highlight markitdown as a 70% token-saver for PDFs/Word/Excel.
  • Brief side note: there are GitHub "star controversies". Repos getting fake engagement from suspicious accounts with no other activity.
