TL;DR
Andrew Warner and guest Peter Cooper walk through this week's most popular open-source AI repos on GitHub. Headliners include a local NotebookLM clone with custom voices, a token-compression tool to fight context blowup, a Claude-based Lovable imitator you can run from your phone, and Apple's new macOS container runtime. Worth a skim for anyone tracking the agent-tooling ecosystem.
Creator's Key Callouts
  • Riley Brown's "rilable" demonstrates how far Claude-powered app generation has come, but Andrew explicitly says don't replace Lovable with it yet — use it as a preview of what's now possible.
  • Peter pushes back on taste-skill: you're trading one set of design clichés (default Claude styling) for another (their curated styles) — the real win is forking the skill files and building your own design system.
  • On the "Phantom Stars" fake-engagement controversy: a third-party tool has flagged several trending repos for suspicious star patterns. Peter and Andrew are not accusing anyone, but it's a real industry issue (and apparently stars can be bought for as little as $1 per thousand).
  • Peter recommends the "last30days-skill" and Agent-Reach as a complementary pair: the skill produces a finished deliverable, while Agent-Reach is the surgical underlying tool you can use inside any workflow.
